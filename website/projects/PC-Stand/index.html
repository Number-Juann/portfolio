PC Stand

Project Overview

This is a simple and low‑cost PC stand designed in SolidWorks and manufactured using 3D‑printed end blocks and wooden dowels. It elevates the PC to improve airflow to the power supply, helping prevent heat buildup on carpeted or enclosed surfaces. The design prioritizes ease of assembly, low material cost, and clean aesthetics.

Engineering Challenges & Solutions

The PC’s power supply draws air from the bottom of the case to cool internal components. On hard surfaces, the built‑in case clearance is usually sufficient. However, on carpeted floors the PSU intake becomes blocked, restricting airflow and causing the power supply to run hotter than intended.

To solve this, the stand elevates the PC enough to restore proper airflow to the PSU fan, ensuring consistent cooling performance and reducing long‑term thermal stress on the system.

  • Designed and modeled in SolidWorks, including precise dimensional measurements to ensure proper fit with the PC case.
  • Implemented interference pressure‑fit joints, enabling assembly without hardware, adhesives, or fasteners.
  • Integrated mixed‑material manufacturing, combining 3D‑printed components with hardware‑store dowels for a low‑cost, accessible build.
